üèÜ World Championship - The Biggest Event in Esports Viewership

The League of Legends World Championship (Worlds) is the annual pinnacle of competitive LoL. First held in 2011, it has grown to become the most-watched esports event globally, with the 2022 finals reaching over 5 million peak concurrent viewers.

Teams qualify through regional leagues (LCK, LPL, LEC, LCS, and emerging regions), competing in a month-long tournament. South Korean and Chinese teams have dominated, with T1 (formerly SKT) winning four titles and Faker becoming the most decorated player in LoL history.

About League of Legends

The world's most popular competitive game

üìñ History & Impact

League of Legends launched in 2009, developed by Riot Games. Inspired by the original Dota mod, LoL streamlined the MOBA formula while maintaining strategic depth. It quickly became the most played PC game globally, revolutionizing free-to-play gaming.

Riot pioneered the franchised league model in esports, creating stable regional competitions with salaried players. The LCK, LPL, LEC, and LCS form the four major regions, with teams competing for slots at international events like MSI and Worlds.

Iconic moments include Faker's rise to GOAT status with four World titles, the historic rivalry between SKT and Samsung, and underdog runs like 2022's DRX championship.
