üìú History & Evolution

League of Legends launched in 2009 and quickly became the world's most popular PC game. Riot Games built a franchised esports ecosystem unmatched in scale, with regional leagues feeding into international tournaments.

The Franchised Era

Early Days (2010-2012) saw grassroots tournaments and the first World Championships. Season 1 Worlds featured an $100,000 prize pool‚Äîmodest by today's standards but groundbreaking then.

LCS Revolution (2013+) introduced salaried leagues in North America and Europe. Teams received stability, players got contracts, and viewership exploded. Korea's OGN (later LCK) set the competitive standard.

Global Franchising (2017+) brought permanent partnership leagues. LCS, LEC, LCK, and LPL became the four major regions with multi-million dollar buy-ins and long-term investments.

Korean Dominance

South Korean teams won Worlds from 2013-2017 with SKT T1 (now T1) claiming three titles. The LCK's infrastructure, training culture, and individual talent established Korea as the premier region. T1's Faker became esports' most recognizable face.

Regional Rise

China's LPL emerged as a powerhouse, with teams like Invictus Gaming, FunPlus Phoenix, and EDward Gaming winning Worlds. The region combines Korean coaching with aggressive playstyles and massive investment.

üí∞ Betting Markets

League of Legends is one of the most popular esports for betting, with extensive market coverage and consistent scheduling throughout the year.

Match Winner

Best-of-3 or best-of-5 series winner. Korean and Chinese teams typically favored internationally.

Map Handicap

Map spread betting. Strong teams often sweep weaker opponents in regional play.

First Blood

Which team gets first kill. Heavily influenced by jungle matchups and early game strategies.

First Tower

Which team destroys first tower. Strong indicator of early game control and lane priority.

First Dragon/Baron

Objective-based betting. Dragon stacking is crucial in current meta.

Total Kills

Over/under on combined kills. Varies by team playstyle‚Äîsome teams play slow, others force fights.

Betting Tips

Regional Strength: LCK and LPL teams are significantly stronger than Western teams at international events. Adjust expectations accordingly.

Patch Timing: Major patches before tournaments can upset established hierarchies. Teams that adapt quickly gain edges.

Player Form: Star players can carry games. Track individual performance across multiple matches before betting.
