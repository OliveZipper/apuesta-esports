üìú History & Evolution

The First Franchised Esports League

When the Overwatch League launched in 2018, it was revolutionary. City-based franchises, minimum player salaries ($50K+), benefits, and production quality that rivaled NFL broadcasts. Teams like the London Spitfire, New York Excelsior, and San Francisco Shock became household names.

6v6 to 5v5 Transition

Overwatch 2 brought a seismic change: the shift from 6v6 to 5v5, removing one tank role. This fundamentally altered strategies, hero metas, and team compositions. The change accelerated gameplay but divided the community.

Korean Dominance

South Korean players and coaching staff have dominated Overwatch esports. The mechanical precision, team coordination, and infrastructure from Korea's gaming culture created an edge that Western teams struggled to match.

ü¶∏ Hero Roles

Tank (1 per team)

The anchor of the team. Tanks create space, absorb damage, and enable their team. Heroes like Reinhardt, D.Va, Winston, and Sigma define team compositions.

Damage (2 per team)

The fraggers. DPS players secure kills and carry fights. Hitscan (Widowmaker, Ashe), projectile (Pharah, Hanzo), and flankers (Tracer, Genji) offer different playstyles.

Support (2 per team)

Healers who keep the team alive. Main supports (Ana, Mercy) focus on healing output, while flex supports (Zenyatta, Lucio) provide utility and damage.
