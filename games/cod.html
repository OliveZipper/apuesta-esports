About Call of Duty Esports

Call of Duty has been a cornerstone of console esports since the mid-2000s. The franchise transitioned to a franchised city-based league model in 2020 with the Call of Duty League (CDL), featuring 12 permanent teams with multi-million dollar buy-ins from major esports organizations and traditional sports owners.

Unlike most esports, CoD releases a new title annually, meaning the competitive meta completely resets each year. This creates unique dynamics where teams that dominated one title might struggle with the next, and new stars can emerge quickly. The current format features 4v4 gameplay across multiple game modes.

Competitive Format

The CDL uses a structured format that mirrors traditional sports leagues:

  • Team Size: 4 players + substitutes per franchise
  • Game Modes: Hardpoint, Search & Destroy, Control
  • Match Format: Best of 5 maps (HP, S&D, Control, HP, S&D)
  • Season: Regular season + Majors + Championship
  • Majors: 4 major tournaments throughout the season
  • CDL Points: Accumulate throughout season for playoff seeding

Major Tournaments

The CDL season features several high-stakes events:

  • CDL Championship (Champs) - Season-ending championship, $2.5M prize pool
  • CDL Majors - 4 major LAN events per season ($500K each)
  • CDL Online Qualifiers - Regular season matches for Major seeding
  • Challengers - Path-to-pro circuit for amateur players
  • All-Star Weekend - Exhibition event featuring top performers

CDL Franchises

The 12 franchised teams represent major cities across North America and Europe:

  • OpTic Texas - The most popular org in CoD history
  • Atlanta FaZe - Backed by FaZe Clan, multiple championships
  • Los Angeles Thieves - 100 Thieves franchise
  • Toronto Ultra - Competitive NA franchise
  • New York Subliners - NYXL ownership group
  • London Royal Ravens - Premier European franchise
  • And more: Minnesota, Seattle, Boston, LA Guerrillas, Vegas, Miami

Betting Markets

CoD betting offers extensive markets due to its multi-map format:

  • Match Winner - Overall series result
  • Map Winner - Individual map outcomes
  • Map Handicap - Spread on maps won
  • Total Maps - Over/under on maps played
  • First Map Winner - Opening map (always Hardpoint)
  • Tournament Winner - Outright Major/Championship winner
  • Player Props - Individual K/D, kills per map

Betting Tips

  • Mode Specialists: Some teams excel at specific modes (HP vs S&D)
  • Map Vetoes: Understanding team map pools is crucial
  • New Titles: Early season is volatile as teams adapt
  • LAN vs Online: Home crowd advantage matters in CDL
  • Head-to-Head: Certain matchups consistently favor one team
  • OpTic Factor: Matches involving OpTic draw different energy/pressure
